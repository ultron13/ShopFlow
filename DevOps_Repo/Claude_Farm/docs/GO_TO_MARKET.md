# FarmConnect SA — Go-To-Market Plan

**Last Updated:** May 2026  
**Corridor:** Limpopo → Gauteng (Tomatoes, Months 1-6)

---

## 1. Launch Strategy — Tomato Corridor

### Why This Corridor First

- Limpopo produces 350-400K tonnes of tomatoes annually — abundant, consistent supply
- Gauteng has 7,000-9,000 independent restaurants — large, reachable buyer base
- Price spread (R5.50/kg farm gate → R20-25/kg restaurant) is large enough to build a business in the gap
- N1 highway is well-established; refrigerated transport exists and is hireable
- ZZ2 and other major cooperatives in Tzaneen are organised and reachable

### Target Launch Segments

**Supply side:** Established cooperatives with existing cold storage and at least 5,000 kg/week available.  
Priority: ZZ2, Mahela Fresh Produce, Limpopo Tomato Growers Association, Mogalakwena Cooperative

**Demand side:** Independent restaurants (not chains) in Sandton, Rosebank, Melrose with:
- 30+ covers
- Weekly fresh produce spend R6K-R15K
- Not locked into a long-term supplier contract

## 3. Farmer / Cooperative Acquisition

### Approach

Farmers don't respond to cold outreach or marketing. They respond to:
1. A peer they trust vouching for the platform
2. Guaranteed orders (not promises — actual orders already placed)
3. Seeing payment land in their account

### Onboarding Sequence

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Field agent makes personal introduction at cooperative meeting | Field Agent |
| 2 | Demonstrate: show an existing confirmed order on the platform | Field Agent |
| 3 | Offer zero commission for first 3 months | Ops |
| 4 | Walk through listing creation in person | Field Agent |
| 5 | First collection — field agent present for the full process | Field Agent |
| 6 | First payment lands — cooperative posts about it in WhatsApp groups | Organic |

### Priority Cooperative Partners (Month 1)

| Cooperative | Location | Size | Contact Approach |
|-------------|----------|------|-----------------|
| ZZ2 | Mooketsi / Tzaneen | Large commercial | Direct outreach to commercial director |

| Limpopo Tomato Growers Assoc. | Provincial | Industry body | Request member meeting presentation |
| Mogalakwena Cooperative | Mokopane | Smallholder | Community liaison through extension officer |

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Launch Pricing (Months 1-3)

| Party | Rate | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Seller commission | 0% | Trust-building; rises to 5% after Month 3 |
| Buyer commission | 6% | Rises to 8% after Month 3 |

### Delivered Price Breakdown

```
Farm gate (Limpopo):          R5.50/kg
Logistics & handling:         R4.50/kg
FarmConnect fee (launch):     R1.40/kg  (6% buyer, 0% seller blended)
Quality/grading:              R0.45/kg
Buffer/shrinkage:             R4.15/kg
─────────────────────────────────────
Delivered price to buyer:     R16.00/kg

Restaurant was paying:        R20-25/kg
Buyer saving:                 20-36%
```

### Pricing Governance

- Prices reviewed weekly against NAMC fresh produce market data
- Farm gate price is fixed per listing — no dynamic pricing in MVP
- If farm gate price rises > 10% in a week, notify all standing order buyers before the change takes effect

## 6. B2B Retention

**The standing order is the moat.** Once a chef has set up a weekly order and trusts the quality, switching back to a market agent is friction. Protect the standing order with:

- Proactive quality alerts ("this week's batch is exceptional — Grade A uniformity 96%")
- Delivery time reliability (in by 07:00, no exceptions)
- One WhatsApp message away from adjusting quantity
- Invoice arrives same day as delivery — makes their bookkeeping easy

**Churn triggers to monitor:**
- Two quality disputes in one month
- Late delivery two weeks in a row
- Buyer goes silent on WhatsApp

## 7. B2G Pipeline (Month 9-12)

### Target Buyers

| Segment | Value | Entry Point |
|---------|-------|------------|
| National School Nutrition Programme | R9.5-10.2B/yr national | Provincial Education Department tenders |
| Municipal hospitals | R1-2B/yr | Supply Chain Management departments |
| Correctional Services | R500M/yr | National tender |

### B2G Sales Motion

B2G procurement is not sold — it's tendered. The sales motion is:

1. **Build relationships** with procurement officers in Gauteng provincial departments (Month 6-9)
2. **Pilot proposal:** Offer a 3-month pilot on a small contract to demonstrate capability
3. **Audit trail:** Ensure every transaction is documentable and exportable before engaging
4. **B-BBEE Level 1:** Non-negotiable for government procurement — must be in place
5. **Respond to RFQs/tenders:** Monitor National Treasury eTender Portal daily from Month 9

Government sales cycle: 6-12 months from first contact to first purchase order. Start conversations in Month 6, target signed pilot by Month 12.
